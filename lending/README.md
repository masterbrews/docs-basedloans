## What makes lending work

#### ✔ You choose which tokens you back

You are not matched to every token on the platform. You pick the tokens you are willing to accept as collateral and set a USDC cap for each one. If you are not comfortable holding a token, do not enable it. Your settings stay in effect until you change them, and you can update them at any time.

#### ✔ No liquidation management

There are no liquidation bots, no real-time price monitoring, and nothing you need to do mid-loan. Once your USDC is matched to a loan, it is locked for the term and the outcome is determined at expiry, either the borrower buys back or they do not. You do not intervene either way.

#### ✔ Two clear paths to yield

When a borrower buys back, you receive your USDC plus your share of their fee. When a borrower defaults, you receive the collateral tokens they deposited. Those tokens were worth roughly twice the loan value at the time the loan was made, so even significant price declines give you room to come out ahead. Both outcomes pay you, just in different forms.

#### ✔ Unmatched USDC is always yours

Any USDC sitting in your deposit that has not yet been matched to a loan can be withdrawn at any time. There is no lockup on unmatched capital. Only matched USDC, which is actively funding a live loan, waits until the loan resolves.

#### ✔ 50% LTV is your buffer

Every loan is made at 50% loan-to-value. A borrower posting $2,000 in collateral receives $1,000 in USDC. If they default, you receive $2,000 worth of tokens at the price used when the loan opened. The token price has to fall more than 50% from open before your return drops below what you lent. That buffer is baked in at the protocol level and cannot be changed per-loan.
